The analysis of healthcare indicators for the Northern Mariana Islands reveals significant variation across different metrics. The Primary Healthcare Institutions Ratio shows the highest value, indicating robust infrastructure. In contrast, the Electronic Medical Record Adoption Rate is the lowest, suggesting an opportunity for digital advancement. Overall, the metrics do not follow a linear trend but rather exhibit a peaked shape due to substantial fluctuation between categories. Notably, the Vaccination Rate demonstrates a strong upward trend, marking it as an area of improvement. The analysis suggests that while there are strengths in healthcare infrastructure, areas such as digital record adoption and health insurance require further development to ensure comprehensive healthcare coverage.
