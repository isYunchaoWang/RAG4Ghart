Over the period from 1973 to 1990, driver satisfaction for Long-Haul Operators consistently increased, showing a linear growth trend from approximately 60 to over 120 on the index. In contrast, Freight Train satisfaction decreased from 1973 until around 1982-1983, reaching a low under 20, before rebounding with a significant growth phase that nearly returned to its initial level by 1990. The period for Freight Train was marked by a pronounced decline followed by recovery, indicating a significant growth rate post-1982 and a distinct turning point at the trough of this satisfaction index. Overall, Long-Haul Operators maintained a steady improvement, whereas Freight Trains experienced a cyclic pattern with a notably impactful recovery phase.
