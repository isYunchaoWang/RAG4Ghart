From 1950 to 1966, customer traffic across four restaurants showed a consistent upward trend. The Emerald Diner experienced the highest customer numbers throughout the period, though the growth rate was moderate compared to Stellar Bistro, which exhibited the steepest growth trajectory after initial modest increments. Stellar Bistro surpassed others around 1956 and continued maintaining the lead until 1966. In contrast, The Rusty Kettle and Crimson Plate progressed at a steadier pace, with The Rusty Kettle displaying slightly more dynamic growth patterns despite fluctuations. Overall, the competitive landscape shifted notably as Stellar Bistro accelerated past peers to become the most frequented establishment.
