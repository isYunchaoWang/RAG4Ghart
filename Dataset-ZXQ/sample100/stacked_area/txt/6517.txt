The data on delivery time from 1970 to 1997 shows a general declining trend. The maximum delivery time was over 14 days in the early 1970s, while the minimum was under 6 days in the early 1990s. On average, delivery time decreased over the years, reflecting improvements in logistics and technology. The trend follows a pattern of decline with periodic fluctuations, notably with significant decreases in delivery times around the early 1980s and 1990s. Autonomous vehicles have emerged prominently over the period, contributing significantly to these improvements. Overall, the data indicates a clear trend towards faster delivery methods, driven by technological advancements and optimization of logistics strategies.
