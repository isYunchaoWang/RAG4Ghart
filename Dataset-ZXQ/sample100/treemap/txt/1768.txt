In 2024, the cultural activities landscape is predominantly driven by artwork sales, accounting for 63.68% of activities. The primary market, encompassing art fairs and galleries, represents a substantial 47.80%, with notable contributions from regional art fairs (14.61%) and traditional galleries (11.85%). Secondary market activities, such as auction houses, comprise a smaller 15.88%. Meanwhile, cultural activities make up 36.32% of the distribution, with public art and exhibitions sharing similar importance, at 18.28% and 18.04% respectively. Within public art, community projects are a strong focus (12.91%), while online exhibitions, led by NFT galleries, highlight the significant digital shift. Overall, this distribution reflects a balanced interplay between traditional and emerging cultural formats, with a notable emphasis on digital innovation and public engagement.
