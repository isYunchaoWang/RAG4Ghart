The Global Tourism Value Chain analysis reveals significant variations in tourist preferences across different continents and attractions. Africa emerges as the leading source continent with the highest tourist contribution totaling 3,130 million, whereas Europe records the lowest with 113 million. The total tourist numbers across all continents amount to 17,983 million, with an average of 2,995 million tourists per continent. The distribution showcases a fairly stable trend of tourists favoring three main types of attractions: theme parks, cultural festivals, and shopping districts. Notably, cultural festivals attract the most tourists, indicating a strong global interest in cultural experiences. Overall, there are no dramatic fluctuations, but a steady preference for diverse tourist attractions is evident.
