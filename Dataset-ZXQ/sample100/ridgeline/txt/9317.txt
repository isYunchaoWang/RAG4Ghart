The analysis of housing price fluctuations across different regions reveals varied patterns. Metropolitan areas exhibit stable pricing with slight fluctuations, indicating consistent demand. Urban redevelopment and coastal areas showed moderate volatility, with prices peaking toward the middle of the timeline before stabilizing. Coastal properties experienced the highest growth initially, before declining. Suburban and rural areas demonstrated a generally stable trend with minimal fluctuations. Mountain and luxury residences had pronounced initial price hikes, followed by gradual declines. Overall, the data suggests a dynamic housing market, with peak price trends concentrated in the center of the observation period, indicating market corrections and adjustments across regions.
