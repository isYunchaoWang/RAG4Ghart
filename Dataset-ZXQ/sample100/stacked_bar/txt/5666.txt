The sales revenue in the food and beverage industry from the years 2000 to 2017 demonstrates a fluctuating yet generally increasing trend. In 2000, the industry generated substantial revenue with snacks leading early growth. A significant peak is observed around 2005, where both snacks and bakery products combined reached the highest recorded revenues. Following a dip in 2010, a gradual increase is marked from 2014 to 2017, highlighting an overall positive growth trajectory. The data reveals noteworthy boosts in both sectors after 2005, with bakery sales notably expanding their share in later years, signifying a shift towards diversified revenue sources within the industry.
