The transportation distribution data for 2021 highlights distinct patterns in how different categories contribute to the overall transportation infrastructure. Rail freight stands out as the most utilized category with a substantial share of 24.23%, indicating a strong reliance on railways for transporting goods. This is closely followed by the collective contribution of road freight and highway networks at 18.69% each, underscoring the critical role of road systems. Urban mobility is also significant, with its largest contributors being public transit (15.99%) and bus systems (8.7%). Micromobility options like bike sharing and e-scooters, while more minor individually, collectively help reduce congestion. The data shows a balanced distribution across various categories with diverse options supporting the overall transportation network.
