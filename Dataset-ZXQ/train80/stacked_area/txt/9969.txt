The analysis of cross-border delays from 1972 to 1989 reveals a general upward trend in delay hours, with occasional fluctuations. The data shows a gradual increase in disruptions over time, with both political and weather-related factors contributing to the delays. Notable growth points occur around the early 1980s and mid-1980s, indicating possible turning points likely influenced by significant external events. Overall, the trend is characterized by steady growth with some variations, reflecting the increasing impact of both political and weather disruptions on cross-border activities over the analyzed period.
