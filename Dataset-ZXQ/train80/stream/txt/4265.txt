From 1987 to 1999, the overall trend in average treatment costs shows a steady increase. The cost fluctuates with periods of growth and slight declines, but exhibits a generally upward trajectory, indicative of rising healthcare expenses. Rehabilitation Therapy and Emergency Procedure costs are prominent contributors to this growth, showing the most noticeable increases over time. The most significant growth occurs between 1994 and 1999, suggesting a potential rise in demand or changes in healthcare practices affecting these categories.
