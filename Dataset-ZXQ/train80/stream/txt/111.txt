The analysis of fertilizer prices from 1966 to 1985 shows that overall prices have experienced an upward trend with minor fluctuations. The maximum pricing level occurs towards the end of the period around 1984, while the minimum is observed at the start of the period in 1966. There is a notable growth phase during the late 1970s to early 1980s, indicating a period of rising prices across various products. Key turning points include fluctuations around the mid-1970s and a more stable increase post-1980. The trend is characterized by gradual increments rather than abrupt changes, reflecting a steady market growth with consistent demand across different product categories. GeoNuts and Skyberry Pulp contributed significantly to the rising trend, with other products following suit, highlighting an overall prosperous market environment for fertilizers during these years.
