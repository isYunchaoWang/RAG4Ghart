From 1981 to 1995, natural gas prices exhibited an overall increasing trend. There were noticeable fluctuations, but the trend was primarily characterized by gradual growth. The values ranged from the lowest around 1986 to the highest in 1995. The price increases were consistent, indicating a relatively steady rise without significant abrupt changes. The period saw periods of growth following some minor fluctuations, suggesting that external factors might have contributed to short-term dips. By 1995, the prices reached their peak, marking a steady upward trajectory over the assessed years.
