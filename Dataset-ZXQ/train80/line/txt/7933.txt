The analysis of average watch time from 1961 to 1967 reveals varied trends across different user types. New Users display the highest watch times, peaking around mid-period and slightly decreasing towards the end, indicating an initial growth followed by stabilization with slight decline. Mobile Users have exhibited a consistent linear growth throughout the years, achieving the largest growth rate, suggesting increased engagement or preference for mobile viewing. Free Users have shown a moderate increase over time, with no drastic shifts or declines. Desktop Users' watch times remain the lowest but have experienced a gradual linear growth, indicating slowly increasing engagement. Overall, the data highlights a shift towards mobile usage as the primary mode of content consumption, while New Users' engagement reached its maximum earlier in the timeline. There are no sharp turning points, but rather consistent trends with slight nuances over the observed period.
