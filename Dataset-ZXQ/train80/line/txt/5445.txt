The analysis of home sales data from 1978 to 1989 for Starfall Mart highlights a cyclical pattern with notable fluctuations. The maximum monthly sales occurred in 1980, 1984, and 1988, with sales units peaking over 1100. The minimum figures were observed in 1982, 1986, and 1989, dropping below 700 units per month. The trend indicates a repeating cycle of sharp decline followed by equally steep growth, producing a series of peaks and troughs. Despite the significant fluctuations within this period, no overarching increasing or decreasing trend is evident; the pattern follows a consistent cyclical format. The most significant growth phase can be seen between 1986 and 1988, where sales surged from less than 700 units to over 1100 units, marking the highest growth rate in the data set and demonstrating a remarkable recovery phase. Overall, the data suggests a stable but oscillating sales pattern driven by external factors influencing demand.
