The sales revenue in the food and beverage industry experienced notable fluctuations from 2000 to 2019. The maximum revenue occurred in 2015, while the minimum was seen in 2006. The total revenue over the period shows an overall increasing trend with significant variability year to year. The overall pattern indicates a cyclical trend with peaks in 2000, 2013, and 2015, suggesting growth after several periods of decline or stabilization. There were significant spikes in revenue, particularly between 2010 and 2015, indicating high growth during this phase. Additionally, the years following 2015 saw a slight decline and stabilization, showing a turning point after the peak in 2015. The analysis reveals that despite the fluctuation, the industry demonstrated incremental growth, primarily driven by the meat and frozen segments.
