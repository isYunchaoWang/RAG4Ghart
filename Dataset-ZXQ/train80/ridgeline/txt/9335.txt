The analysis of housing price fluctuations across different areas reveals distinct patterns. Suburbs experience a notable peak followed by a gradual decrease, suggesting a significant rise and subsequent stabilization. Coastal properties show a steady rise, indicating a consistent increase without major fluctuations. Luxury residences demonstrate a mid-point peak, followed by gradual stabilization, reflecting an earlier surge in prices. Rural areas exhibit a pronounced peak toward the latter half, indicating recent significant growth. Metropolitan areas show gradual growth with minimal fluctuations, suggesting steady increase. Mountain regions experience a central peak, reflecting moderate fluctuations. Urban redevelopment areas present an initial rise and fall, followed by stabilization, indicative of an early price surge. Overall, the data implies diverse trends in housing prices, with suburban and rural areas showing significant recent growth, while coastal and metropolitan areas maintain more consistent upward trends.
