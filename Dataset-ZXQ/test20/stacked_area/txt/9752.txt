From 1971 to 1993, cargo shipments experienced significant growth, with a consistent upward trend across all services: Continental Freight, Oceanic Carriers, and Parcel Services. The total shipments increased steadily, reflecting a linear growth pattern with a more pronounced acceleration in the late 1980s and early 1990s. Parcel Services demonstrated the highest growth rate during this period. Key turning points occurred around 1985 and 1990, where noticeable increases in shipment volumes are evident, suggesting an expansion in logistics demand and service capacity. Overall, the data indicates robust growth and evolving service contributions within the shipping industry over these two decades.
